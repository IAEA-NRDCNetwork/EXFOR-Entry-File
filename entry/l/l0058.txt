METHOD     (EXTB,RINGR) Direct photoneutron detection. Ring-      L005800100019 
           ratio neutron multiplicity measurement. A noteworthy   L005800100020 
           feature of this experiment is the use of a remotely    L005800100021 
           controlled pneumatic 8-position revolving sample       L005800100022 
           changer, which allowed all of the samples, as well as  L005800100023 
           a blank sample and a neutron calibration source, to    L005800100024 
           be inserted into the center of 4pi neutron detector    L005800100025 
           sequentially without changing the beam tuning or any   L005800100026 
           other experimental conditions. This feature enabled    L005800100027 
           relative cross section measurements to Be made with a  L005800100028 
           precision of about 2%. This is much better than the    L005800100029 
           overall absolute accuracy of about 5% typical of       L005800100030 
           recent measurements near the peak of the giant         L005800100031 
           resonance of medium and heavy nuclei.                  L005800100032 
ANALYSIS   Photoneutrons from each sample and from a              L005800100033 
           sample-blank were measured, both with and without the  L005800100034 
           annihilation target present. After the recorded        L005800100035 
           neutron events were corrected for pileup in the        L005800100036 
           detector, the neutron backgrounds (annihilation target L005800100037 
           removed) were substracted for both the positron and    L005800100038 
           electron measurements.                                 L005800100039 
           Because the energy spectrum of the annihilation-plus-  L005800100040 
           bremsstrahlung radiation differs from that of purely   L005800100041 
           bremsstrahlung photons, a measured correction was      L005800100042 
           applied to the ion-chamber (beam monitor) response to  L005800100043 
           normalize the electron data to the positron data.      L005800100044 
           The normalized electron data then were substracted     L005800100045 
           from the positron data, leaving the neutron counts     L005800100046 
           which result only from the annihilation radiation.     L005800100047 
           The measured sample-blank data, normalized to equal    L005800100048 
           photon flux, were substracted from the target data to  L005800100049 
           give the net target results.                           L005800100050 
           After a small correction for the neutron multiplicity  L005800100051 
           in each ring, the data were corrected for the energy   L005800100052 
           dependent efficiency of the neutron detector using the L005800100053 
           ring-ratio result for each energy.                     L005800100054 
           The data were converted to photoneutron cross          L005800100055 
           sections by applying the measured ion-chamber          L005800100056 
           response per photon, the known number of target        L005800100057 
           nuclei in the beam and a small correction for the      L005800100058 
           photon attenuation in the sample.                      L005800100059 
           A multiplicity analysis of the number of neutrons      L005800100060 
           recorded in each beam burst allows the (gamma,n)       L005800100061 
           and (gamma,2n) yields to Be extracted simultaneously   L005800100062 
           and independently.                                     L005800100063

COMMENT    An interesting result is the complete absence of  any  L005800100070 
            (gamma,2n) cross section for 238U and 234U. The valuesL005800100071 
            of nu prompt(e) for 234U agree with those measured    L005800100072 
           with neutrons incident on 233U. The parameters  of the L005800100073 
           giant dipole resonance deduced from the total          L005800100074 
           photonuclear cross sections show that these  nuclei    L005800100075 
           have  large  static deformations,  as expected. The    L005800100076 
           integrated photofission cross sections are large (as   L005800100077 
           are the absolute fission probabilities),  and account  L005800100078 
           for 60 to 80% of the total photonuclear absorption     L005800100079 
           strength.                                              L005800100080
