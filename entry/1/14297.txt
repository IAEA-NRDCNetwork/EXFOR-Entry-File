           efficiency is determined in two ways. The first is to  14297  1   15 
           assume the accurately known value of nubar for 235U    14297  1   16 
           at thermal energy. The detector efficiency is the      14297  1   17 
           mean number of neutrons detected per gate divided by   14297  1   18 
           the known value of nubar. Another method is to         14297  1   19 
           replace the fission detector with proton recoil        14297  1   20 
           neutron detector such as hydrogen-filled proportional  14297  1   21 
           counter that is inserted into the center of liquid     14297  1   22 
           scintillator in place of the fission counter. Then     14297  1   23 
           monoenergetic neutrons are passed through the          14297  1   24 
           proportional counter, and the gate opened with pulses  14297  1   25 
           produced by recoil protons. Every proportional         14297  1   26 
           counter pulse is associated with the scattering of a   14297  1   27 
           neutron out of the beam and into a liquid              14297  1   28 
           scintillator by a hydrogen nucleus in the counter.     14297  1   29 
           For fission spectrum, about 98.5% of the neutrons are  14297  1   30 
           captured in the detector.                              14297  1   31
