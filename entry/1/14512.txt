DETECTOR   (GE) A 65% relative efficiency, Mirion BEGe detector   1451200100015 
           was set up horizontally shrouded on four-sides by a    1451200100016 
           copper-lined open lead box. The detector was placed    1451200100017 
           inside the lead shield to reduce room background       1451200100018 
           observed by the detector and the copper liner          1451200100019 
           attenuated x-rays emitted by the lead shielding.       1451200100020 
SAMPLE     (92-U-235,ENR=0.9984) Two isotopically-characterized   1451200100021 
           targets of high-purity (99.84+ 0.03%) 235U-oxide. Two  1451200100022 
           targets, one large and one small, were prepared at     1451200100023 
           LLNL from the stock powder by loading nominal masses   1451200100024 
           of 63.0 and 8.6 milligram-equivalents of 235U-metal    1451200100025 
           into separate quartz ampoules. Quartz wool was packed  1451200100026 
           into the quartz tube to x the 235U-oxide in-place      1451200100027 
           within the ampoule before heat sealing them.           1451200100028 
METHOD     (ACTIV) Two ampoules along with a witness foil         1451200100029 
           package were loaded into the center of the Godiva      1451200100030 
           critical assembly and exposed to a single pulsed       1451200100031 
           irradiation. The witness foil package contained        1451200100032 
           high-purity copper, gold, cobalt, iron, and titanium   1451200100033 
           wires. Activities observed in these wires were used    1451200100034 
           to estimate the neutron fluence of the exposure. The   1451200100035 
           targets were retrieved from Godiva shortly after the   1451200100036 
           irradiation. The small target was placed 8.8 cm from   1451200100037 
           the face of the BEGe detector for list-mode gamma      1451200100038 
           counting 230.2 minutes after irradiation and was       1451200100039 
           later replaced by the larger target 290.77 minutes     1451200100040 
           after irradiation. Data were taken for a total of 7    1451200100041 
           days. The switch between small and large targets was   1451200100042 
           made to increase the data acquisition rate; thus,      1451200100043 
           improving the statistical precision of fission product 1451200100044 
           measurements made at days-after-irradiation.           1451200100045 
ANALYSIS   (DECAY) Spectra were analyzed using Mirion's           1451200100046 
           Genie2000 software. Spectra loaded into Genie2000      1451200100047 
           were analyzed using a step-based process. First, the   1451200100048 
           spectra were analyzed using a second-difference peak   1451200100049 
           search algorithm. Next, distances between peaks were   1451200100050 
           compared against a user-defined threshold based on     1451200100051 
           the measured resolution calibration to identify        1451200100052 
           single peaks and multiplets. After sorting, peaks      1451200100053 
           were fit using the sum of a low-tail Gaussian          1451200100054 
           function and either a linear or step based background  1451200100055 
           estimation function, depending on which background     1451200100056 
           function gave better residual chi** 2. Peaks within    1451200100057 
           multiplet regions were fit simultaneously for better   1451200100058 
           background estimation. Each peak fit was visually      1451200100059 
           reviewed and in many cases manually refit using the    1451200100060 
           Mirion's Interactive Peak Fitting software; this       1451200100061 
           utility was crucial in providing reliable data         1451200100062 
           quality because of the complexity encountered within   1451200100063 
           the spectrum. Decay data used in the final             1451200100064 
           measurements were taken from Nudat 2.0 in 2011.        1451200100065 
CORRECTION A first pass analysis was conducted using only         1451200100066 
           first-order Bateman decay corrections to identify      1451200100067 
           cases where higher order Bateman corrections were      1451200100068 
           needed. Specific activities corrected to a concurrent  1451200100069 
           time are expected to be similar. Second-order Bateman  1451200100070 
           decay corrections were generally applied in cases      1451200100071 
           where the parent nuclide's half-life was >5% of the    1451200100072 
           daughter's half-life as was the case for 134Te/I. The  1451200100073 
           last correction was the self-shielding correction      1451200100074 
           factor.                                                1451200100075
