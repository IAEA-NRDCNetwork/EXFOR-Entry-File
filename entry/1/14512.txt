DETECTOR   (GE) A 65% relative efficiency, Mirion BEGe detector   14512  1   15 
           was set up horizontally shrouded on four-sides by a    14512  1   16 
           copper-lined open lead box. The detector was placed    14512  1   17 
           inside the lead shield to reduce room background       14512  1   18 
           observed by the detector and the copper liner          14512  1   19 
           attenuated x-rays emitted by the lead shielding.       14512  1   20 
SAMPLE     (92-U-235,ENR=0.9984) Two isotopically-characterized   14512  1   21 
           targets of high-purity (99.84+ 0.03%) 235U-oxide. Two  14512  1   22 
           targets, one large and one small, were prepared at     14512  1   23 
           LLNL from the stock powder by loading nominal masses   14512  1   24 
           of 63.0 and 8.6 milligram-equivalents of 235U-metal    14512  1   25 
           into separate quartz ampoules. Quartz wool was packed  14512  1   26 
           into the quartz tube to x the 235U-oxide in-place      14512  1   27 
           within the ampoule before heat sealing them.           14512  1   28 
METHOD     (ACTIV) Two ampoules along with a witness foil         14512  1   29 
           package were loaded into the center of the Godiva      14512  1   30 
           critical assembly and exposed to a single pulsed       14512  1   31 
           irradiation. The witness foil package contained        14512  1   32 
           high-purity copper, gold, cobalt, iron, and titanium   14512  1   33 
           wires. Activities observed in these wires were used    14512  1   34 
           to estimate the neutron fluence of the exposure. The   14512  1   35 
           targets were retrieved from Godiva shortly after the   14512  1   36 
           irradiation. The small target was placed 8.8 cm from   14512  1   37 
           the face of the BEGe detector for list-mode gamma      14512  1   38 
           counting 230.2 minutes after irradiation and was       14512  1   39 
           later replaced by the larger target 290.77 minutes     14512  1   40 
           after irradiation. Data were taken for a total of 7    14512  1   41 
           days. The switch between small and large targets was   14512  1   42 
           made to increase the data acquisition rate; thus,      14512  1   43 
           improving the statistical precision of fission product 14512  1   44 
           measurements made at days-after-irradiation.           14512  1   45 
ANALYSIS   (DECAY) Spectra were analyzed using Mirion's           14512  1   46 
           Genie2000 software. Spectra loaded into Genie2000      14512  1   47 
           were analyzed using a step-based process. First, the   14512  1   48 
           spectra were analyzed using a second-difference peak   14512  1   49 
           search algorithm. Next, distances between peaks were   14512  1   50 
           compared against a user-defined threshold based on     14512  1   51 
           the measured resolution calibration to identify        14512  1   52 
           single peaks and multiplets. After sorting, peaks      14512  1   53 
           were fit using the sum of a low-tail Gaussian          14512  1   54 
           function and either a linear or step based background  14512  1   55 
           estimation function, depending on which background     14512  1   56 
           function gave better residual chi** 2. Peaks within    14512  1   57 
           multiplet regions were fit simultaneously for better   14512  1   58 
           background estimation. Each peak fit was visually      14512  1   59 
           reviewed and in many cases manually refit using the    14512  1   60 
           Mirion's Interactive Peak Fitting software; this       14512  1   61 
           utility was crucial in providing reliable data         14512  1   62 
           quality because of the complexity encountered within   14512  1   63 
           the spectrum. Decay data used in the final             14512  1   64 
           measurements were taken from Nudat 2.0 in 2011.        14512  1   65 
CORRECTION A first pass analysis was conducted using only         14512  1   66 
           first-order Bateman decay corrections to identify      14512  1   67 
           cases where higher order Bateman corrections were      14512  1   68 
           needed. Specific activities corrected to a concurrent  14512  1   69 
           time are expected to be similar. Second-order Bateman  14512  1   70 
           decay corrections were generally applied in cases      14512  1   71 
           where the parent nuclide's half-life was >5% of the    14512  1   72 
           daughter's half-life as was the case for 134Te/I. The  14512  1   73 
           last correction was the self-shielding correction      14512  1   74 
           factor.                                                14512  1   75
