METHOD     The ORNL pile oscillator measures the thermal neutron  11047  1   29 
           capture cross section of a sample by moving the sample 11047  1   30 
           so as to pulse periodically the neutron density        11047  1   31 
           within the pile. It consists of a mechanical system    11047  1   32 
           which oscillates a sample back and forth about once a  11047  1   33 
           second through an annular, neutron-sensitive           11047  1   34 
           ionization chamber, which is located in a constant     11047  1   35 
           and intense neutron flux in the graphite reactor of    11047  1   36 
           the pile. The local depletion of neutrons around the   11047  1   37 
           capturing sample depends on the absorption by the      11047  1   38 
           sample and can be used to compare absorptions. This    11047  1   39 
           transient depletion pulses the ionization chamber      11047  1   40 
           current, and these pulses are amplified, and           11047  1   41 
           integrated by using the rectified pulses to charge a   11047  1   42 
           condenser whose voltage can then be measured. The      11047  1   43 
           ratio of the capture cross sections for two samples    11047  1   44 
           is found from the weights which experimentally give    11047  1   45 
           the same voltage response. In practice all samples     11047  1   46 
           were compared with gold, whose capture cross section   11047  1   47 
           is rather well known.                                  11047  1   48
