METHOD     (COINC) A 10-channel pulse-height analyzer is used to  12337  1   13 
           select a bias for the fission counter which is low     12337  1   14 
           enough to accept nearly all fission events but high    12337  1   15 
           enough to discriminate against alpha particles. About  12337  1   16 
           1500 pairs of fission-triggered and background sweeps  12337  1   17 
           are then recorded photographically on a roll of film.  12337  1   18 
           The minimum acceptable height of neutron pulses is     12337  1   19 
           also marked on the table. In order to avoid sweeps     12337  1   20 
           triggered by events other than fission, only those     12337  1   21 
           traces are read which have a properly located prompt   12337  1   22 
           pulse due to fission gammas and proton recoils; at     12337  1   23 
           least 99 percent of the fission events produce such a  12337  1   24 
           pulse. The number of acceptable pulses after the       12337  1   25 
           prompt-pulse time from each fission and background     12337  1   26 
           trace are then recorded. Such measurements have been   12337  1   27 
           made for fission induced by thermal neutrons and by    12337  1   28 
           80-keV neutrons in U233, U235, and Pu239 and for       12337  1   29 
           spontaneous fission of Pu240, Cm244 and Cf252. To      12337  1   30 
           deduce the emission probabilities, Pn, from the        12337  1   31 
           various numbers of pulses per sweep, it is necessary   12337  1   32 
           to know the efficiency of the detector for fission     12337  1   33 
           neutrons. The experimental efficiencies obtained were  12337  1   34 
           87(2) percent and 85(3) percent, respectively, for     12337  1   35 
           neutrons with energies of 0 to 100 keV and 2 MeV. If   12337  1   36 
           it is assumed that neutron leakage is the primary      12337  1   37 
           reason for change in efficiency with energy, the       12337  1   38 
           Monte Carlo calculations may be used to plot a smooth  12337  1   39 
           curve of efficiency versus energy passing through the  12337  1   40 
           two experimental points. For fission-spectrum          12337  1   41 
           neutrons the average efficiency thus determined is     12337  1   42 
           83(2) percent.                                         12337  1   43
