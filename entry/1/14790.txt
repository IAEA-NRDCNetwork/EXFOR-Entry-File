METHOD     SPIDER was developed for measuring independent FPYs,   1479000100021 
           i.e. fission yields after the prompt neutron emission  1479000100022 
           but before beta-decay, in direct-kinematics using the  1479000100023 
           double energy-double velocity (2E-2v) method. In the   1479000100024 
           2E-2v method, the masses of both fission fragments     1479000100025 
           are determined by measuring their kinetic energy and   1479000100026 
           velocity. This method has several advantages compared  1479000100027 
           to other direct kinematics techniques, like the        1479000100028 
           double-energy (2E) and electromagnetic separation, as  1479000100029 
           it enables the measurement of multiple correlated      1479000100030 
           fission quantities, such as pre and post-neutron       1479000100031 
           emission FPYs with high mass resolution (~1 AMU),      1479000100032 
           total kinetic energy (TKE), and prompt neutron         1479000100033 
           multiplicity. In contrast to the 2E method, the 2E-2v  1479000100034 
           method measures the independent FPY's directly,        1479000100035 
           without relying on theoretical inputs for neutron      1479000100036 
           emission.                                              1479000100037
