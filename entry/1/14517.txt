SAMPLE     (90-TH-232,ENR=0.9999) The 232Th target was comprised  14517  1   12 
           of a deposit of 232ThF(4) on thin carbon backing. The  14517  1   13 
           232Th deposit had a thickness of 195.2+-8.3 mu-g/cm2   14517  1   14 
           and the effective carbon backing was 120 mu-g/cm2 thick14517  1   15 
           The target was tilted 45deg with respect to the        14517  1   16 
           incident beam.                                         14517  1   17 
DETECTOR   (SI) Two arrays of four Si PIN photodiodes were used   14517  1   18 
           to detect the fission products, where the photodiodes  14517  1   19 
           were arranged on opposite sides of the beam at angles  14517  1   20 
           60deg and 120deg from the incident beam. The           14517  1   21 
           individual PIN diodes had an area of 1 cm2, and the    14517  1   22 
           detectors were 2.1 cm from the center of the target.   14517  1   23 
           The timing pulse from a Si PIN diode coupled with the  14517  1   24 
           accelerator RF signal were employed to measure the     14517  1   25 
           time of flight of each interacting neutron. The        14517  1   26 
           uncertainty of the position of the photofission peak   14517  1   27 
           in the TOF spectrum was found to be 2.4%.              14517  1   28 
METHOD     (TOF) Neutron energy was determined by time of         14517  1   29 
           flight. The 625 mu-s macropulse proton beam contains   14517  1   30 
           340 micropulses of width 250 ps that are spaced 1.8    14517  1   31 
           mus apart, where the macropulses had a repetition      14517  1   32 
           rate of 100 Hz. The distance from the spallation target14517  1   33 
           to the Th target was measured to be 1384.9+-1.1 cm.    14517  1   34 
           The 15R beam line with 13.85m flight path was used in  14517  1   35 
           this experiment. The 1.8 mu-s proton pulse rate        14517  1   36 
           theoretically allows for a small amount of             14517  1   37 
           wrap-around neutrons with energy < 0.4 MeV to reach    14517  1   38 
           the scattering chamber among the neutrons of the       14517  1   39 
           previous or trailing pulse. The fission cross section  14517  1   40 
           for 232Th(n,f) at En < .4MeV is small enough that      14517  1   41 
           the slow wrap-around neutrons were ignored.            14517  1   42 
INC-SOURCE (SPALL) Neutron beams were generated from an           14517  1   43 
           unmoderated tungsten spallation source using an        14517  1   44 
           800MeV proton beam. Neutron time of flight and energy  14517  1   45 
           was calculated via the proton accelerator pulse timing.14517  1   46 
CORRECTION Corrections for pulse height defects and fragment      14517  1   47 
           energy loss were applied                               14517  1   48
