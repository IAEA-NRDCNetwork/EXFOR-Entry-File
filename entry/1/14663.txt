METHOD     (EDE) The experiment was performed at LANSCE WNR to    14663001   18 
           investigate the proton channel from the 10B + n        14663001   19 
           system. This experiment was performed in the NZ        14663001   20 
           chamber. The experiment was designed to observe        14663001   21 
           protons up to 20 MeV. The thickness of boron on a      14663001   22 
           tantalum backing was optimized to give less than 0.5   14663001   23 
           MeV energy loss for protons of 0.5 MeV traversing the  14663001   24 
           target layer at 45 degrees, the maximum angle used     14663001   25 
           here, with respect to the normal. The target was       14663001   26 
           located 9.12 meters from the neutron source. During    14663001   27 
           this experiment, a small amount of polyethylene was    14663001   28 
           placed in the beamline to reduce the low energy        14663001   29 
           portion of the incident neutron spectrum. Four de e    14663001   30 
           telescopes at 20, 60, 90, and 135 degrees were used    14663001   31 
           with gas proportional detectors for the de and a       14663001   32 
           silicon detector for the e detector. The solid angles  14663001   33 
           of the detectors were 13.1, 20.4, 26.8 and 16.6 msr,   14663001   34 
           respectively. The neutron flux was monitored with a    14663001   35 
           235U fission chamber one meter downstream of the       14663001   36 
           chamber.                                               14663001   37
