           ** Principal points of this evaluation                 V002900100014 
            (1.) Simultaneous evaluations, in which a group of    V002900100015 
             cross-sections are considered, together with their   V002900100016 
             correlation.                                         V002900100017 
            (2.) Ratio of cross sections is employed to avoid the V002900100018 
             absolute measurement of neutron fluence rate.        V002900100019 
             All activation cross-sections are in fact measured asV002900100020 
             ratios relative to some monitor (standard) cross-    V002900100021 
             sections. All results were firstly adjusted to a     V002900100022 
             neutron energy of 14.70 MeV.                         V002900100023 
            (3.) The cross-sections are determined in a known     V002900100024 
             neutron fluence which may be determined either by a  V002900100025 
             proton recoil telescope, or by a plastic scintillatorV002900100026 
             or by the associated particle technique (in case of  V002900100027 
             D-T neutron source). The associated particle         V002900100028 
             technique is absolute.                               V002900100029 
            (4.) The mathematical treatment follows that of Hayes V002900100030 
             and Axton, Metrologia 6(1969)21.                     V002900100031 
           ** Results                                             V002900100032 
            The results are mostly in agreement within the errors V002900100033 
             with several other evaluation.                       V002900100034 
            The ENDF/B-V ratio of the 2 most commonly used        V002900100035 
             standard cross-sections, Al-27(n,a) and Fe-56(n,p),  V002900100036 
             was 7.5% higher than the ratio of this results.      V002900100037
