METHOD     (TOF) Flight path to neutron detector 717.5+-0.2 cm.   2319900100021 
           (TRN) Transmission. The data taking time per cycle for 2319900100022 
           the empty sample (3 cm thick Pb bremsstrahlung absorber2319900100023 
           only) was 600 s, for the Au and Ta samples it was 900s.2319900100024 
           The total measurement time was about 78 hours.         2319900100025 
           The order of the cycle was empty-Au-Ta-Pb in the first 2319900100026 
           half of the experiment, whereas it was                 2319900100027 
           empty-Au-empty-Ta-empty-Pb in the second half with 300s2319900100028 
           duration for empty.                                    2319900100029 
           The absolute scale of time of flight is determined     2319900100030 
           using the bremsstrahlung peak and known flight path.   2319900100031 
           The flight path has been verified by resonances with   2319900100032 
           strong elastic neutron scattering in 208Pb.            2319900100033 
           In the experiment 110 (130) measurements with Au (Ta)  2319900100034 
           were made and 320 with Pb absorber only.               2319900100035 
FACILITY   (LINAC,2GERZFK) Superconducting Electron Linac.        2319900100036 
            101.5625 kHz micropulse repetition rate.              2319900100037 
INC-SOURCE (BRST,PHOTO) Electrons are accelerated to 30 MeV .     2319900100038 
           The electrons generate bremsstrahlung photons which    2319900100039 
           release neutrons in secondary (gamma, n) reactions on  2319900100040 
           lead.                                                  2319900100041 
INC-SPECT  The energy range extends from about 10 keV to 10MeV .  2319900100042 
           The neutron source strength at the nominal beam current2319900100043 
           was calculated with the Monte Carlo N-Particle         2319900100044 
           Transport Code MCNP-4C3 to be 10**13 neutrons/s .      2319900100045 
           The bunch duration is about 5 ps.                      2319900100046 
DETECTOR   (SCIN) The transmitted neutrons were detected using a  2319900100047 
           plastic scintillator (Eljen EJ-200, 1000mm x 42mm x    2319900100048 
           11mm) that was read out on both ends using high-gain   2319900100049 
           Hamamatsu R2059-01 photomultiplier tubes (PMT).        2319900100050 
           Surrounded by a 1 cm thick lead shield.                2319900100051 
           The detection threshold for recoil protons about 10keV.2319900100052 
           Detector count rate of about 10 kHz (empty sample beam)2319900100053 
           corresponds to a neutron count rate of about 250 n/s.  2319900100054 
           On average, only every tenth accelerator bunch is      2319900100055 
           registered by the scintillator.                        2319900100056 
           The time of flight of the transmitted neutrons was     2319900100057 
           measured in list mode with the Multi-Branch-System     2319900100058 
            (MBS1) real-time data acquisition developed at GSI    2319900100059 
           - with dispersion of 97.6 ps/channel.                  2319900100060
