           Total Absorption Calorimeter (TAC) and three MicroMegas2316200100119 
            (MGAS) detectors.                                     2316200100120 
           -The TAC is a segmented 4PI detector made of 40 BaF2   2316200100121 
           crystals of 15 cm thickness. For detecting with high   2316200100122 
           efficiency the gamma-ray cascades following neutron    2316200100123 
           capture reactions, with typical total energies of      2316200100124 
           5-10MeV and multiplicities up to 6 or 8, depending on  2316200100125 
           the investigated nucleus. The signals from the 40 BaF2 2316200100126 
           crystals are directly connected to 8 bit flash-ADC     2316200100127 
           digitizers (with 8MB memory per channel) sampling at   2316200100128 
           250 MHz, which allow recording 32 ms long data buffers 2316200100129 
           that cover the neutron energy range from relativistic  2316200100130 
           energies down to 0.3 eV.                               2316200100131 
           Detection efficiency of the TAC for fission events was 2316200100132 
           determined for different conditions on deposited energy2316200100133 
           and multiplicity, yielding the values from 38 to 97 % -2316200100134 
           see Table 1 of J,EPJ/A,48,29,2012.                     2316200100135 
           Detection efficiency of the TAC for capture events was 2316200100136 
           calculated accurately by means of Monte Carlo          2316200100137 
           simulations, using code GEANT4, to be 0.70+-0.02 .     2316200100138 
           - MGAS, based on the microbulk principle, is a very    2316200100139 
           particular gas detector with an active volume divided  2316200100140 
           in two regions by a thin micromesh. The ionization     2316200100141 
           produced by FF from 235U takes place in the first      2316200100142 
           region (4 mm, about 1kV/cm) and the electrons produced 2316200100143 
            are multiplied by producing an avalanche in the second2316200100144 
           region (50 microm, about 50 kV/cm).This low-noise      2316200100145 
           detector is very transparent to neutrons and gamma-rays2316200100146 
           ,thus minimizing the background associated to neutron  2316200100147 
           reactions in the structural materials and the          2316200100148 
           absorption of the gamma-rays emitted in the neutron-   2316200100149 
           induced reactions.Efficiency for detecting fission     2316200100150 
           events from the 235U samples in the MGAS was determined2316200100151 
           experimentally to be 0.90+-0.02.                       2316200100152
