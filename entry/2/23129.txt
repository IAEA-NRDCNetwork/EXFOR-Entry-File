METHOD     (REC) Neutron spectrum was measured with proton recoil 23129  1  147 
           technique, at 20 degrees in the lab. system , using    23129  1  148 
           5-mm thick CH2 target.                                 23129  1  149 
           (TOF) TOF - to select peak neutrons and reject tail    23129  1  150 
           neutrons. Fight path of about 4.5 m.                   23129  1  151 
           TOF of each CP from reaction target at the Medley      23129  1  152 
           centre to first SIBAR is subtracted from the total TOF.23129  1  153 
           (EDE) DeltaE-E technique for particle identification   23129  1  154 
           and energy measurement.                                23129  1  155 
           Instrumental background was measured without sample.   23129  1  156 
           TOF FWHM of (n,p) elastic peak at 20 deg. in the lab.  23129  1  157 
           system, measured from CH2 target with 175 MeV QMN,     23129  1  158 
           is equal to 8.8 ns. Repetition time between beam       23129  1  159 
           micropulses is of the order of 45 ns.                  23129  1  160 
DETECTOR   (TFBC) TFBC and IOCH - for relative neutron beam       23129  1  161 
           (IOCH) intensity monitoring, both utilizing neutron    23129  1  162 
           induced fission of U-238.                              23129  1  163 
           (SPEC,TELES,SIBAR,CSICR)                               23129  1  164 
           Medley spectrometer consist of eight three-elements    23129  1  165 
           telescopes mounted in evacuated cylindrical chamber of 23129  1  166 
           90 cm diameter. Each telescope consists of two fully   23129  1  167 
           depleted silicon surface barrier detector (served as   23129  1  168 
           deltaE detector, 1-st 50-60 microm thick, second -     23129  1  169 
           1000 microm thick) and CsI(Tl) scintillator (as E      23129  1  170 
           detector, 50 mm diameter, 100 mm length) with          23129  1  171 
           18x18 mm**2 Hamamatsu S3204-08 photodiode.Calculations 23129  1  172 
           by Geant and PHITS codes showed that all produced light23129  1  173 
           ions fully stop in CsI(Tl) detector.                   23129  1  174 
           Medley can detect protons  from 2 up to 170 MeV,       23129  1  175 
           Thresholds for other ions are higher, up to 9 MeV for  23129  1  176 
           alphas.                                                23129  1  177 
           Exp. data themselves were used for calibration.        23129  1  178 
           The same calibration for all light-ions is applied to  23129  1  179 
           SIBARs, whereas CsI(Tl)s are calibrated independently  23129  1  180 
           for each light-ion.                                    23129  1  181

ANALYSIS   Absolute double-differential cross-sections SIG are    23129  1  212 
           obtained from net counts N, applying the expression 3.523129  1  213 
           of T,BEVILACQUA (=Eq.5 in NIM/A) :                     23129  1  214 
           SIG/N=(SIG-H/I-H)*(2A/ACH2)*(mCH2/m)*(FCH2/F)*         23129  1  215 
                 (OmegaCH2/Omega)*(1/deltaE)                      23129  1  216 
           where A and ACH2 are respectively the atomic weight of 23129  1  217 
           the reaction target and of the CH2 target,             23129  1  218 
           m and mCH2 are the target masses,                      23129  1  219 
           F and FCH2 are the neutron fluences measured with one  23129  1  220 
           of the neutron monitors, OmegaCH2/Omega is the ratio   23129  1  221 
           between the solid angle seen by the telescope at 20    23129  1  222 
           degrees and the telescope used for the measurement,    23129  1  223 
           deltaE is the energy bin width,                        23129  1  224 
           I-H -integral of a Gaussian fit of high energy elastic 23129  1  225 
           peak provides the measured number of protons induced   23129  1  226 
           by 175MeV neutrons.                                    23129  1  227 
           Production of protons from carbon was measured at TSL  23129  1  228
