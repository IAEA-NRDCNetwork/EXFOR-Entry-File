METHOD     (PHD  ) For each analyzed fission event four quantities2215200100014 
            are recorded, the anode pulse heights and the electron2215200100015 
            drift times from each side of the chamber, from which 2215200100016 
            the fragment mass and energy can be determined provid-2215200100017 
            ing that the non-ionizing part of the energy lost by  2215200100018 
            the fragment (the so-called pulse height defect) is   2215200100019 
            properly estimated. This was investigated by consider-2215200100020 
            ing those fission events for which there is perfect   2215200100021 
            fragment identification (so-called 'cold' fission,    2215200100022 
            characterized by high kinetic energy fragments and no 2215200100023 
            neutron emissioN). The resulting pulse height defect  2215200100024 
            was shown to be negligible for the present configur-  2215200100025 
            ation, thus the fragment kinetic energy can be well   2215200100026 
            represented by a linear function of anode pulse height2215200100027 
            response.                                             2215200100028 
DETECTOR   (IOCH ) Twin ionization chamber working in pure methane2215200100029 
            gas at 450 +-1 mbar pressure, consisting of two back- 2215200100030 
            to-back gridded chambers separated by a central cath- 2215200100031 
            ode at the center of which is located the fissile     2215200100032 
            sample. Two Frisch grids of 30 micron tungsten wire   2215200100033 
            screen the anodes from the positive ion effect. The   2215200100034 
            chamber is operated at constant room temperature, and 2215200100035 
            the gas filling replenished at a flow rate of 5 l/hr. 2215200100036 
            The energy resolution was approximately 200 keV for a 2215200100037 
            100 MeV fragment kinetic energy.                      2215200100038
