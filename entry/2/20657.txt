ANALYSIS   .Because of the low energy resonances at 0.321 eV      20657  1   33 
            and 0.461 eV of Eu-151, the contribution of these     20657  1   34 
            resonances to the resonance integral (it is about     20657  1   35 
            80%) will be reduced when a cadmium filter            20657  1   36 
            is used (cadmium-cut-off-energy = 0.55 eV).           20657  1   37 
           .In order to measure the total resonance integral      20657  1   38 
            (from an energy greater than about 0.2 eV) a          20657  1   39 
            new method is developed (see ref. JIN,36(1974)1447)   20657  1   40 
            which is based on irradiations in two different       20657  1   41 
            neutron spectra with known neutron temperature        20657  1   42 
            and epithermal index.                                 20657  1   43 
           .The neutron temperature was measured using the        20657  1   44 
            neutron temperature detector Lu-176 (for these        20657  1   45 
            measurements see thesis DM-6909).                     20657  1   46 
           .The epithermal component was measured using           20657  1   47 
            Au-197 and Co-59 as monitors.                         20657  1   48 
           .The S0-factor (Westcott formalism) was calculated     20657  1   49 
            from the relation S0=(g2*(r1/r2)-g1)/(F1-(r1/r2)*F2)  20657  1   50 
            where r,g,F are the induced activity,the g-factor and 20657  1   51 
            the epithermal index of the irradiation positions     20657  1   52 
            1 and 2, respectively.                                20657  1   53 
           .For comparison the cadmium method  has been also      20657  1   54 
            used.                                                 20657  1   55
