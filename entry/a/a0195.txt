           the radionuclide yield by the following formula:       A0195  1   26 
              W=0.554 x Za x T/2 x (B/Pc)      (1)                A0195  1   27 
           where W is the reaction yield in atoms per 1000 part.; A0195  1   28 
                 Za is the relative a charge of the bombarding    A0195  1   29 
                                                     particle;    A0195  1   30 
                 T/2 is the nuclide's half-life in days;          A0195  1   31 
                 B is the radionuclide yield in MBq/(uA-h);       A0195  1   32 
                 Pc is the content of target isotope, in %.       A0195  1   33 
           If radionuclide formed through several reactions for   A0195  1   34 
           which various stable isotopes of the irradiated elementA0195  1   35 
           serve as target nuclei. In many cases it is possible   A0195  1   36 
           both to identify the reaction which makes the largest  A0195  1   37 
           contribution to the nuclide yield and to determine the A0195  1   38 
           yield of this reaction. The yield of the main reaction A0195  1   39 
           calculated by the formula                              A0195  1   40 
             Wo =(Wn x 100 - W1 x P1 - W2 x P2 - ...) (3)         A0195  1   41

                    (atoms/l000 protons) calculated from the      A0195  1   43

                    of formula (1) for Pc = 100%;                 A0195  1   45
