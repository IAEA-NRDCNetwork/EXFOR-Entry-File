COMMENT    Abstract. Incomplete fusion reactions were             A0441  1   12 
           investigated by measuring the excitation functions of  A0441  1   13 
           nine evaporation residues in 16-O + 51-V reaction in   A0441  1   14 
           the beam energy 4-6 MeV/amu, using the well-known      A0441  1   15 
           recoil catcher technique and gamma-ray spectrometry.   A0441  1   16 
           The experimental data were compared with that obtained A0441  1   17 
           from MONTE CARLO simulation calculations using the     A0441  1   18 
           PACE2 code. The results indicate the presence of       A0441  1   19 
           incomplete fusion process in the production of two     A0441  1   20 
           alpha emission products. This was further confirmed by A0441  1   21 
           the measurement of recoil range distribution of these  A0441  1   22 
           isotopes at 96 MeV beam energy. Calculations of the    A0441  1   23 
           average angular momentum associated with these productsA0441  1   24 
           revealed the peripheral nature of these incomplete     A0441  1   25 
           fusion reactions.                                      A0441  1   26 
           /Special remark by authors/. In case of cobalt productsA0441  1   27 
           the experimental data are much higher than the         A0441  1   28 
           calculated data indicating the contribution from       A0441  1   29 
           incomplete fusion reactions. These incomplete fusion   A0441  1   30 
           reactions can be of the 51-V(16-O,8-Be)59-Co* type.    A0441  1   31 
           The excited 59-Co* nucleus may subsequently de-excite  A0441  1   32 
           by neutron emission to give 58-56 Co. In the case of   A0441  1   33 
           the evaporation residues 61-Co, there may be a breakup A0441  1   34 
           of 16-O into 12-C and 4-He and subsequent incomplete   A0441  1   35 
           fusion of 12-C in the reaction 51-V(16-O,A)63-Cu; the  A0441  1   36 
           evaporation of two protons leads to the formation of   A0441  1   37 
           the residues. It was found that the incomplete fusion  A0441  1   38 
           contributions with respect to the complete fusion      A0441  1   39 
           cross-sections increase from 3% at 60 MeV to about 20% A0441  1   40 
           above 90 MeV.                                          A0441  1   41
